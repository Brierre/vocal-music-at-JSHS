Family

It's just me, my two boys, my mom, and our cat, Loki. He's a good cat.

Sugar Valley Community Band

I have been with the Sugar Valley Community Band for several years. My sister, Ms. Crust, is the director of the group, and it has been a family affair with our kids in the group at one point in time or another. This band is a very tight-knit group of musicians from a tiny place over the mountain. They're small, but mighty! We perform at community events year-round. My roles have included: trumpet, clarinet, oboe, and currently French horn, and I have even had to fill in as conductor once!

Central Mountain Marching Band

I'm also a director of the Central Mountain Marching band, along with my sister and our percussion instructor, Mr. Mazzulla. I am going into my 8th year of directing this group, serving as the show-writer and band front instructor, both skills I have picked up along the way in my time there.

My Software Development Journey

Right now, I am studying software development, mostly just to acquire a new skill, although I probably wouldn't turn down a job in development making double my teaching salary. I have already completed a back-end development boot camp through Promineo Tech and the Community College of Philadelphia learning Java, MySQL, Spring Boot, APIs, Menu-Driven Applications, and so much more. Currently, I am enrolled in the front-end development boot camp, again through Promineo Tech and Gateway Community College and learning JavaScript, HTML5, CSS, Bootstrap, JQuery, React, and other skills. I really enjoy development using all of these skills, but I have said all along I think I am a true back-end developer. This website is an example of using HTML5, CSS, and Bootstrap that I have created.

Development Projects

Some of the projects I have created are located in my GitHub. So far, part 1 of Rehearsal Scheduler is complete. I have created the card game, WAR, using Java, and then JavaScript. I have made a Tic-Tac-Toe game, and I am working on a few others. I have also created websites for the KCSD Alumni Band and my own page you see here. Look for more projects to come this summer as I learn more skills.

Kayaking

Since the first time I got in a kayak in a New Jersey creek and was attacked by a territorial goose, I was hooked. I bought rails to put on my car, and as soon as I get them installed, I'm buying my own kayaks and rack. Some of my favorite kayak journeys were 13 miles on a too-shallow Pine Creek with my sister, Ashley, a moonlight kayak paddle of the loop at Pocomoke River State Park, and kayaking in the Atlantic Ocean at Grand Turk.

Crochet

I love to crochet. I go in waves where I crochet everything and then don't crochet at all. I always said if I teach at just one building I will start a crochet club. This year might be my chance. I especially love crocheting funny hats and little figures from my favorite video game series, Final Fantasy, but when I have a lot of time, I like granny square projects.

My Favorite Games

My favorite game is an MMO called Final Fantasy XI. Despite it being more than 20 years old, it is still up and running on servers that connect people worldwide. I contribute to an open source emulator project, writing code to emulate the game, which is where I got the brainy idea to learn to code "for real". I also loved playing World of Warcraft, and I'm a big fan of the Civilization and Age of Empires games.
